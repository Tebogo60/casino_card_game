# Cassino Cards

## More Than a Game

In the streets and corners of South African townships, before smartphones and streaming, there was Cassino. Played on stoeps, in backyards, on overturned crates, wherever two people had a deck of cards and time to burn. It needed no board, no dice, no electricity. Just cards, a flat surface, and a sharp mind.

Cassino has been passed down through generations not because it is simple, but because it is deep. Grandparents, parents, and children have all sat across from each other with the same 40 cards, and every game has told a different story.

---

## It Is Not About What You Have

That is the first thing every new player learns the hard way.

You can be dealt the perfect hand and still lose. You can hold the cards that should win the game and watch your opponent dismantle your plan move by move. Cassino does not reward the lucky, it rewards the patient, the calculating, the ones who think three moves ahead while pretending they are not thinking at all.

Every turn is a decision. Place a card and you are giving your opponent information. Build and you are making a promise you have to keep. Capture too early and you leave the table open. Wait too long and your opponent takes what was yours.

There is no hiding in Cassino. Every move says something.

---

## What the Game Tests

- **Memory** — knowing what has been played, what is still out there, what your opponent might be holding
- **Planning** — building towards a capture while protecting your own builds
- **Reading people** — knowing when your opponent is setting a trap and when they are bluffing with nothing
- **Discipline** — resisting the easy move when the right move is harder

It is the kind of game where a quiet player at the corner who looks like they are barely paying attention is the most dangerous person at the table.

---

## For Everyone

Cassino belongs to no age group. A ten-year-old can learn it in twenty minutes. A sixty-year-old who has played for decades will still find new situations that test them. The rules are few. The depth is endless.

This is that game, brought online, so the corner comes with you.
